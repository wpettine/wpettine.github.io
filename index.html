About me

How do we make complex decisions when the facts are ambiguous? I investigate this question by analyzing neural data, building computational models and designing behavioral tasks. In particular, I investigate in how decision-making is impacted in conditions like  obsessive compulsive disorder and autism. My scientific interests include neural networks, reinforcement learning, systems neuroscience and computational psychiatry. At present, I am a postdoctoral researcher with John Murray at the Yale School of Medicine.

You will find more about my academic background on my CV.
